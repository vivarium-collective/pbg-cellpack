Crowded Cytoplasm

Multi-component molecular crowding in a cellular volume

Four molecular species of different sizes (large complexes at r=40, medium enzymes at r=25, small metabolites at r=15, and tiny cofactors at r=8) are packed into a 600³ nm box simulating cytoplasmic crowding. This demonstrates cellPACK's ability to handle polydisperse mixtures with realistic volume fractions.

Synaptic Vesicle Lumen

Uniform neurotransmitter packing in a small volume

Hundreds of small neurotransmitter-sized spheres (r=5) are packed tightly into a 200³ nm box representing the interior of a synaptic vesicle. A small number of larger transporter proteins (r=20) are interspersed. This showcases cellPACK's efficiency with large molecule counts and tight packing.

Binary Sphere Packing

Two-component size-ratio packing study

A binary mixture of large (r=50) and small (r=15) spheres is packed into a 500³ nm box. The 3.3:1 size ratio allows small spheres to fill the interstices between large spheres, a classic granular-packing problem. This configuration is useful for studying packing efficiency as a function of size ratio.
