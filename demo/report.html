Gradient-Biased Packing

Exponential spatial gradients drive non-uniform molecular placement

Molecules are placed under the joint influence of two exponential gradients along the X and Y axes (70/30 weighting). The result is a concentration hotspot near the low-X / low-Y corner of the bounding box, mimicking biological gradients such as morphogen fields or chemotactic cues. A second, larger species is packed uniformly for contrast.

Polydisperse Size Distributions

Continuous and discrete radius distributions in a crowded volume

Two molecular populations are packed with variable radii. Population A draws radii from a uniform distribution (15-30 nm), modelling the natural size variation in macromolecular complexes. Population B samples from a discrete set of radii (30, 35, 40, 45, 50 nm), representing distinct oligomeric assemblies. cellPACK's size_options feature generates these polydisperse packings in a single pass.

Receptor-Ligand Partner Binding

Proximity-biased packing with stochastic binding probability

Receptor molecules (blue, r=25 nm) are placed first with high priority. Ligand molecules (orange, r=12 nm) then pack using cellPACK's closePartner mode with a 70% binding probability, preferentially placing each ligand near an existing receptor. A background population of small inert molecules fills the remaining volume. This models multivalent binding, immune synapse formation, and other proximity-driven assembly.
